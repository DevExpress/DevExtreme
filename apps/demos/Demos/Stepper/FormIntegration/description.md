This demo shows the integration of DevExtreme Stepper, [Form](/Documentation/Guide/UI_Components/Form/Overview/), and [MultiView](/Documentation/Guide/UI_Components/MultiView/Overview/), including validation for both Form and Stepper.
<!--split-->

Certain fields in the Form, such as *Check-in* and *Check-out* dates, are mandatory in the first step. You cannot proceed to the next step without completing these fields. Attempting to do so renders the first step [invalid](/Documentation/ApiReference/UI_Components/dxStepper/Configuration/items/#isValid), and the [DateRangeBox](/Documentation/Guide/UI_Components/DateRangeBox/Getting_Started_with_DateRangeBox/) component used to select the date range also fails validation. This issue arises because the DateRangeBox and the first step belong to the same [validation group](/Documentation/ApiReference/UI_Components/dxValidationGroup/). The `moveNext` function and Stepper's [onSelectionChanging](/Documentation/ApiReference/UI_Components/dxStepper/Configuration/#onSelectionChanging) event handler trigger a validation check through the `getValidationResult` function. Thus, both clicking the "Next" button and selecting the next step prompt validation for the Form and Stepper.

Once you complete a step, its validity changes its appearance. A valid step displays a check icon, while an invalid one displays an exclamation mark and turns red.
